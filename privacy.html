Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains how Fraud Trace collects, uses, stores, and protects your information.

1. Information We Collect

When you use our website, we may collect information including:

  • Full Name
  • Email Address
  • Phone Number
  • Mailing Address (if provided)
  • Details of the reported incident
  • Uploaded documents and screenshots

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide to:

  • Review your complaint.
  • Contact you for additional information.
  • Improve our reporting process.
  • Maintain records of communications.

3. Evidence You Upload

Documents, screenshots, receipts, chat logs, and other evidence submitted through our website are stored securely and are only used in connection with your complaint.

4. Information Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may disclose information if required by law or with your authorization where appropriate.

5. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative and technical safeguards to help protect the information submitted through our website. While no online system can guarantee absolute security, we take steps to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.

6. Cookies

Our website may use cookies or similar technologies to improve functionality and understand website usage.

7. Third-Party Services

Our website may rely on trusted third-party services, such as email delivery providers or web hosting companies, to operate our services.

8. Your Rights

Depending on applicable laws, you may request access to, correction of, or deletion of the personal information you have provided to us.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions regarding this Privacy Policy, please contact us.
